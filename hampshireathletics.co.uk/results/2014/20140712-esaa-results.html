English Schools' Athletic Association
Track & Field Championships 2014
11-12 July at Alexander Stadium, Birmingham

Congratulations to all the athletes who represented Hampshire & Vectis Schools at the ESAA Track & Field Championships. Although, compared with 2013, fewer medals were won, the teams gained more points and had a better all-round performance with more standards, PBs and number of athletes scoring at least one point.

Individual medals went to:

  • GOLD (7): Oli Bromby (IB 100m); Owen Richardson (IB 400m); Holly Mills (JG Long Jump)
  • SILVER (8): Kai Mumford (JB Hammer); Andrew Douglas (IB Pole Vault); Sam Adams (SB Long Jump); Josie Czura (JG 1500m); Ellie Hodgson (JG Shot); Devon Brimecome (IG 300m Hurdles); IG 4x100m Relay Team; Fiona Brown (SG Shot);
  • BRONZE (3): Richard Allen (SB 3000m); Emma Cowell (SG High Jump); Amber Try (SG Pole Vault).

Altogether, 38 athletes finished in the top eight of their events, including 7 4th's, 5 5th's, 3 6th's, 5 7th and 4 8th's. Including those set in heats, 35 athletes achieved National Standards and 28 achieved Entry Standards, while 21 athletes managed Personal Bests or Season's Best.

And Hampshire has four Internationals: Oliver Bromby, Owen Richardson, Andrew Douglas and Devon Brimecome.

51 athletes contributed to team points for Hampshire including the relays.

Eileen Williams,
Hampshire Team Manager

In addition, Jack Leerson (2nd, JB Javelin), Stanley Livingston (2nd, SB 400m Hurdles) and Danny Ray (3rd, IB 1500m) gained top three places in their events while representing the Channel Islands.
